About Odey

Actuarial Science & Mathematical Statistics Graduate

I am an aspiring actuary and statistical researcher currently pursuing an MSc in Mathematical Statistics at the University of the Witwatersrand. My dissertation explores a Multidimensional Climate Risk Scorecard using a Bayesian Spatial Copula Framework for Insurance Pricing and Sustainability Strategy in KwaZulu-Natal — combining my passions for spatial statistics, Bayesian methods, and actuarial risk modelling into research with real-world climate and insurance applications.

With a BSc in Actuarial Science (where I passed 7 professional actuarial examinations under the Institute and Faculty of Actuaries / Actuarial Society of South Africa framework) and a BSc Honours in Mathematical Statistics completed with Distinction (final mark of 84% on my research project), I bring a unique combination of rigorous quantitative training and applied analytical skill. My technical expertise centres on the R programming environment — including tidyverse, ggplot2, caret, and spatial packages like gstat and sp — supplemented by strong SQL, advanced Excel, and Power BI capabilities.

Beyond coursework, I have demonstrated the ability to work under pressure in competitive settings (ASA DataFest 2025 participant, selected as Facilitator for DataFest 2026), deliver top-performing survey designs (Applied Sampling), and communicate complex statistical concepts to both academic and industry audiences. I currently serve as a Mathematical Statistics Tutor at Wits, teaching first- to third-year students in probability, statistical inference, and regression — strengthening both my own understanding and my ability to make complex ideas accessible. I am a SAADP (South African Actuarial Development Programme), Siyandisa Achievers, and ProMaths Programme alumnus, and I scored 99% in Mathematics and 100% in Physical Sciences at NSC level.
